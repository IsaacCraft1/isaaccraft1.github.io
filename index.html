How to Play Table Tennis

Core Rules of the Game

  • The Service: The ball must be tossed at least 16cm straight up from an open palm and struck so it bounces once on your side, clears the net, and bounces on the opponent's side.
  • Scoring Points: Games are played up to 11 points. A player scores a point when the opponent fails to make a valid return.
  • Alternating Service: Service alternates between players every 2 points until the game ends or reaches a 10-10 deuce.

Standard Grip Styles

Most players use either the Shakehand Grip (similar to shaking hands with the handle) or the Penhold Grip (holding the paddle like a pen), depending on their playstyle preferences.

Why Table Tennis is Beneficial

Physical & Mental Advantages

  • Cardiovascular Health: The fast paced nature of rallies spikes your heart rate, improving stamina and burning calories efficiently.
  • Joint Friendly Exercise: It offers excellent aerobic conditioning with minimal load on your knee and ankle joints.
  • Cognitive Sharpness: Tracking high speed spins forces fast decision making, which sharpens mental focus, reaction time, and spatial awareness.
  • Hand-Eye Coordination: Calculating velocity and timing counter attacks stimulates brain activity.
